Health Risks vs. Demographics

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks, Age & Income

The findings above show positive correlations between certain risk factors like smoking and obesity with poverty and general household income. More specifically, states with a lower average household income have higher obesity rates and higher smoking rates. On the other hand, states with a higher average household income, had higher rates of healthcare coverage and lower rates of obesity and smoking.

In addition, the findings above show a slight correlation with higher median age groups and higher rates of heathcare coverage. While, on the other side of the spectrum, lower median age groups have lower rates of smoking and obesity.
